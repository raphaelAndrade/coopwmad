LinkedIn

What is LinkedIn?

LinkedIn is a professional social networking platform. It allows you to look for jobs, for companies to find new employees and for you to stay connected and up-to-date about your industry.

When searching for a job, having a strong LinkedIn profile is just as important as a resume.

Attention-grabbing Headline

Here are a few examples to give you some inspiration: There’s no rule that says the description at the top of your profile page has to be just a job title. Use the headline field to say a bit more about how you see your role, why you do what you do, and what makes you tick.
  • Google Certified Android Developer
  • Front-end Developer | React Expert | User Experience Designer
  • Web Developer & Front-end Developer with expertise in React.js
  • Software Developer | Skilled in Popular Front-end Libraries | React Blogger

A Summary that Sells

Your LinkedIn summary depends on your objective. Explain your passion and how it drives your goal, or how you help others succeed. Tell a brief professional story or achievement (give examples). It is much more personal than your resume objective.

Here is an idea:
The first thing to say about your LinkedIn summary is – make sure you have one! It’s amazing how many people still leave this field blank when creating their LinkedIn profile. Your summary is your chance to tell your own story – so don’t just use it to list your skills or the job titles you’ve had. Try to bring to life why those skills matter – and the difference they can make to the people you work with. Don’t be afraid to invest some time, try a few drafts, and run your summary past people you know. This is your most personal piece of content marketing – and it’s worth the effort.
Here’s another example:

First and foremost, I love writing code. Ever since writing my first program in Python and manipulating it to produce a desired output, I have been obsessed with the idea of using software to solve practical problems. Software engineering is a never-ending puzzle that I am passionately engaged in solving. I believe in the power of programming to transform and improve the lives of people around the world.

For my first professional contract, I was given the opportunity to lead a full UI/UX overhaul of a small start up's website. Only a few quick years later, I was leading a team at Sling TV working on dynamic content injection via a micro-frontend architecture. Today, I am a software engineer at Dreamstage, a rapidly growing startup looking to define the live event streaming space. As one of the first full time engineers on the team, I wear many hats and get the opportunity to learn new technologies which enable us to scale. We currently use a Next.js + graphQL architecture, leveraging Static-Site Generation as well as Server-Side Rendering to render content-heavy pages at lightning speed.

Even when I leave the office, I tend to continue writing code at home. Most recently I have been working on a website for my band, Collidoscope. I play the piano/synthesier and I find a beautiful crossover between my software and my music. Both pursuits challenge so many aspects of the intellect: creativity, organization, sequential processing, problem solving. This is what keeps me up at night, a never ending thirst to create beautiful, powerful things and share them with the world.

Skills/Interests: JavaScript, React, TypeScript, Next, Express, Node, Redux, Webpack/Babel, graphQL, StyledComponents, CSS, HTML, Git, GitLab, SQL, PostgreSQL, TDD with Jasmine, jest, Cypress, wdio, ingnx, Docker

Skills

Don’t skip this section! You can add up to 50 skills. It will help recruiters find you based on the skills on your profile and you will appear in more searches each week! LinkedIn will also recommend jobs to you, based on the skills on your profile.

  • Look at the skills on someone who has a job you would like to do
  • Look at the skills and requirements on a job description that interests you
  • You should also ask friends, coworkers and classmates to endorse your skills (confirm that they are true). This adds A LOT of credibility to your profile.

Experience and Education

Your Education and Experience sections should reflect what is on your resume.

  • List your current and previous jobs, in reverse chronological order.
  • Find the exact company with the correct logo if you can.
  • List all of your relevant experiences that relate to your goal.
  • List your top 3-5 responsibilities that showcase your best achievements, not everything.

Recommendations

Show your experience

  • Use action words
  • Nowhere in your job description should it say "responsible for..." or "duties included". Say what it is you do, using action words such as managed, led, grew, reduced, saved etc.
  • Use the correct tenses
  • This means use the present tense for your current job and past tense for previous jobs. This may seem like common sense, but it doesn't always happen.
  • “Tested and updated existing software and using my own knowledge and expertise made improvement suggestions.”
  • “Redesigned the company's web-based application and provided beneficial IT support to colleagues and clients.”
  • Awarded Employee of the Month twice for performing great work.
  • Increased sales by 12% in the first year following my promotion
