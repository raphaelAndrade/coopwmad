Formal greeting:
If you know the name of the hiring manager, great!
If you don’t know their name, here are some simple and appropriate options:
Dear [Insert Name]
For the attention of [the Hiring Manager]


Never use these salutations in a cover letter:

Hi,
Hello,
Dear Sir/Madam

Opening paragraph – the HOOK!

A positive and personable introduction
Mention the job title and the company
Enthusiasm for the job
Note that your resume is included (a call to action!)
Never use these in your opening paragraph:

I’m [insert name]
How are you?
I am a co-op student

An example of a BAD opening paragraph:
Hi,
I’m Kayley. I’m a co-op student at cornerstone. I’m interested in the job of Sales Accosiate. I am studying Customer Service and I have also worked in retail in my own country so I know about sales and good customer service. I worked for my company for 3 years.

An example of a GOOD opening paragraph:
For the attention of the Hiring Manager,

I am writing to express an interest in the position of Sales Associate at The North Face. With over 3 years of direct experience in retail and sales for a well-known fashion brand in my previous employment, I would be delighted to continue my professional development with a company such as The North Face. My resume is included for your consideration.

Background

Why are you a good fit for the company?
Why the company is a good fit for you?
Show your knowledge of the company
I don’t have experience in hotels but…
I want to work in sales

Skills

Look at the job description – what are they looking for in a candidate?
Look at your skills – how to they match the job description?
Evidence and example of 2 – 3
Show your knowledge of the company

I am a good team player
I am a hard worker
I know about hospitality
